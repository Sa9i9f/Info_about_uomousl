information about some colleges of the University of Mosul

College of Medicine

Mosul College of Medicine is the second oldest medical college in Iraq after Baghdad College of Medicine. The college was established in July 1959. The Ministry of Health took over the administration of the College of Medicine in Mosul for a short period. The college was subsequently attached (in 1960) to the University of Baghdad and remained so until 1967 when it became part of the University of Mosul. The college received the first meal of the students (thirty male and female students) in 1959, then the number increased successively. The college played a distinguished role in community service for the northern governorates in general and the city of Mosul in particular, as it welcomed high school graduates in those governorates according to the central distribution of the ministry. The College of Mosul College also contributed to the academic teaching of the medical colleges in Erbil, Sulaymaniyah, Tikrit, and finally Duhok. The college has also participated in the teaching of students of the College of Dentistry, Nursing, Pharmacy and Nineveh medicine to date, as well as, clinical services for patients in government hospitals. The college has academic and scientific connections to the rest of the medical community’s colleges in Iraq and outside Iraq, as the college and its graduates have a distinguished and distinguished position with other colleges, knowing that the college is internationally recognized and almost since its foundation The college grants a bachelor’s degree in medical sciences, surgery, and gynecology to students of initial studies, and postgraduate degrees in various medical sciences for graduate students. This is in addition to the facilities it provides for medical research projects at both the basic and clinical levels.

College of Engineering

The College of Engineering was established in 1963 as part of the University of Baghdad and began studying there in the Department of Civil Engineering. Then the Department of Electrical Engineering was added at the beginning of the establishment of the University of Mosul in 1967. Other departments were added to it, such as the Department of Mechanical Engineering and then Agricultural Machinery Engineering (currently Water Resources Engineering) and the Department of Architectural Engineering, and in 1998 the Department of Computer Engineering was opened and finally the Department of Mechatronics in 2005, bringing the total of engineering departments in the college to seven departments. Graduated from the College of Engineering was established in 1963 linked to the University of Baghdad order to create specialized engineering staff that contribute to the overall development and urban renaissance in the country Beginner in the Department of Civil Engineering. In September 1964, the Department of Electrical Engineering was added. After the establishment of Mosul University on the first of April 1967, the College of Engineering became one of its colleges, and in September 1967 it was decided to open new departments such as the Department of Mechanical Engineering, the Department of Architecture and the Department of Agricultural Engineering (Water Resources Engineering) Currently), in September 1998, the Department of Computer Engineering was opened, and in 2005 a college since its inception and until now, with various specializations, 14311 graduates from initial studies, and the total of graduate graduates totaled 905 graduates (PhD and Master).

College of Computer Science and Mathematics

The founding of the College of Computer Science and Mathematics was established on the date of 28/7/1999 by establishing scientific and technical courses On the date of 1999, the evening study was started in the Department of Computer Science. The College has now published two scientific journals: 1- The Iraqi Journal of Statistical Sciences, which is a semi-annual magazine, and issued the first issue of the magazine on 5/7/2001. 2- Al-Rafidain Journal of Computer Science and Mathematics, which is a semi-annual journal and issued the first issue of the magazine on August 18, 2004.
